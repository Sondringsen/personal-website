# My Favorite Books
When I was young I used to read a lot and really enjoyed it. As I got older my interest shifted more towards gaming and other nonsensical things. I blamed my lack of reading on having to read a lot in school. Although somewhat true it was more of a cope. I still continued to read some books, and lately I have been picking it more up. I have curated a list of my favorite books. As you will see the they are quite diverse; some of them are famous classics, some are popular science, and others yet are fantasy. I do not claim to be an expert in literature, but I know what I like and don't like. Some of these books have left quite an impression on me and changed how I think about life. Others have just been entertaining enough to warrant a spot on the list. Note that the list is in no particular order. I have also included a short paragraph about each book and why I liked it. Some of the paragraphs include brief summaries, but should not contain any spoilers. 

## Books
- Picture of Dorian Gray by Oscar Wilde
- Crime and Punishment by Fyodor Dostoevsky
- Brave New World by Aldoux Huxley
- Eragon by Christopher Paolini
- Harry Potter by J.K. Rowling 

### Picture of Dorian Gray by Oscar Wilde
This is one of the books I have read most recently and the main themes are vanity and morality. The book is about a young man with exceptionally good looks called Dorian Gray. In the first chapters a portrait is painted of Dorian Gray meant to capture and preserve the youth, innocence, and beauty of Mr. Gray. While admiring the portrait, Dorian Gray wishes that the man in the painting should pay the toll of time instead of him. The wish is granted and Mr. Gray maintains his youth, but witnesses the aging of the man in the painting.

One of the most famous quotes from the book is "You will always be fond of me. I represent to you all the sins you have never had the courage to commit".
