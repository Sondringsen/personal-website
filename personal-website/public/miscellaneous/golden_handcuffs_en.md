# Golden Handcuffs

The most important thing in my life is my love and relationship with Ingeborg. She is the one I want to build a family with and spend the rest of my life with. She is a soul that radiates warmth, love, and joy, and I am lucky enough to bask in her light. In many ways, she is the source of my happiness. Very few people can say without deceiving themselves that they are the luckiest man in the world. I can. With this as a backdrop, how do I justify choosing to be away from her so much?

About a year ago, I was given a truly unique opportunity. I was admitted to Stanford with financial support that would cover all expenses related to my studies. I could study at one of the world's very best universities completely free of charge. It was of course something I had worked very hard for and something I very much wanted. Accepting the offer would mean moving to California - a 15-hour flight from Norway - for two years. The obvious downside was that I would not be seeing much of Ingeborg during this time.

One of my greatest fears is having to live my life with all the what-ifs. I therefore chose to accept the offer. I am convinced that Ingeborg is the most important thing in my life, and I justify the decision on the grounds that it is only temporary and that it will make our shared future better than if I had turned it down. Still, it makes me wonder whether I made the choice out of fear of not fulfilling my academic and professional potential. How do I know that I will be able to say no to the next irresistible offer? How do I know that I will be able to make the necessary compromise of deprioritizing my career in order to live the life I want to live with Ingeborg?

For someone who has everything and then some, this may sound shallow and dramatic, but I cannot help feeling that I have managed to find myself in a position where I have allowed myself to be put in golden handcuffs. I truly believe and hope that I am a man who lets love trump the fear of untapped potential when faced with that choice.
